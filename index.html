— The Story
A quiet love letter to vegetables.

For more than two decades, the Pure Veg kitchen has been the heart of the Mango Masala house. It opened in 1998 with a simple idea — that vegetarian food in Ajmer deserved the same care, technique and theatre that the world reserves for everything else.

Our cooks come from across Rajasthan, and they bring with them a quiet, generational kind of knowledge: how a tadka should sound when it lands in the dal, how long a paneer should rest before it meets the tandoor, how to bring out the sweetness in a tomato without losing its temper.

What you eat here is unhurried. The gravies are built in the morning. The breads are pulled to order. The thalis are plated like a quiet little carnival of small bowls. Nothing arrives at your table until it is ready to be there.

— The Story
Borders are smaller than menus.

By 2004, Ajmer was already wandering. The same family that walked into Mango Masala for a thali was, on a different night, looking for something further away — a plate of pasta, a mezze board, a bowl of khao soi. And so, very quietly, Mango Curry opened next door.

The premise has not changed in twenty years. Each kitchen is run by a chef trained inside that tradition — pasta is rolled by hand, sauces are reduced over hours, the wok is kept screamingly hot. We do not Indianise what does not need Indianising. The cumin stays where it belongs.

Order like you are travelling. Start in Beirut. Pass through Naples. End in Bangkok. Or stay in one country and read the menu like a small, very careful novel.

— The Story
Where the family began.

Before there was a restaurant, there was a bakery. Cake Craft opened its doors in 1997 — the year a small family decided to put butter, sugar and a stone oven in the middle of Ajmer and see what would happen. The rest of Mango Masala grew, slowly, around it.

Twenty-six years later, we still mix doughs by hand, cream butter the long way, and refuse to use anything from a pre-mix tin. The croissants take three days. The chocolate is tempered, never melted. The sourdough has a starter that is older than most of our staff.

The Earth Bakery, the second face of this kitchen, leans into bread — wholegrain, slow-rise, deeply imperfect, and on the counter only when it is truly ready. Come early. Come often.

— The Story
A small obsession with crust.

Most kitchens make a pizza. We make crust. Everything else — the sauce, the cheese, the slow-roasted topping that took three hours to caramelise — is in service of the dough underneath. That is the founding belief of this kitchen, and it has not moved since 1998.

The dough cold-ferments for forty-eight hours. The base is rolled, never tossed for show, until it is almost translucent. Each pizza spends ninety seconds against the stone, no longer, no shorter. The result is a crust that is thin, crisp at the rim, with a small chew at the centre — the kind that holds a slice up without folding under it.

Order it Margherita and you'll understand what the kitchen can do. Order it Burrata and you'll understand why we keep the menu small.

About Mango Masala

A premier family restaurant proudly serving Ajmer and beyond since 1998 — where bold flavour meets warm ambiance.

— Our Story
1997

Mango Masala is a premier and authentic family restaurant that has been proudly serving the Ajmer area and beyond since 1998. Before there was a restaurant, there was a bakery. Cake Craft opened its doors in 1997 — the year a small family decided to put butter, sugar and a stone oven in the middle of Ajmer and see what would happen.

Since then, our mission has been to provide high-quality food for all those who wish to combine a fun and enjoyable ambiance with skillful cooking into one extraordinary dining experience. The rest of Mango Masala grew slowly, warmly, around that original oven.

Today, Mango Masala is the happening family restaurant where locals go to dine, celebrate and socialise. Our menu features dishes assembled from the freshest ingredients, sure to bring bold and inspiring flavour into your life. Four kitchens, one family — and a quarter-century of love for this city.
